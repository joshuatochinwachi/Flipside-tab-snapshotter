Introduction
This dashboard delivers a comprehensive behavioral analysis of both DEX Traders and Liquidity Providers within the Avalanche ecosystem. Using the Flipside Intelligence Driven Growth (IDG) methodology, I employed a structured, data-driven approach to unpack user journeys, engagement patterns, platform usage, and liquidity contribution across key DEX platforms on Avalanche over the past 12 months (May 2024 – April 2025).

User Segmentation Framework
I adopted a value-based tiering model to categorize users and liquidity providers into seven distinct wallet tiers based on activity and volume:

  • Whales (Top 1%) – Highest contributors to trading and liquidity volume

  • Sharks (1–5%) – Large regular traders and LPs

  • Dolphins (5–10%) – Mid-level consistent actors

  • Fish (10–20%), Octopus (20–40%) – Moderate and regular users

  • Crabs (40–60%), Shrimp (Bottom 40%) – Small or occasional users

This segmentation was applied consistently across both Traders and LPs, enabling a unified framework for cross-analysis of behavioral patterns and tier-specific performance across trading and liquidity provision.

Analytical Value & Insights
This dashboard offers actionable insights for growth teams, DEX operators, ecosystem foundations, and data analysts by highlighting:

  • Engagement & Behavior: Tracks volume, action frequency, retention, and cohort trends across user tiers

  • Liquidity Dynamics: Measures LP-specific metrics including pool participation, average volume per LP, and DEX-specific behaviors

  • Retention & Churn: Quantifies how well users and LPs are retained over time, including cohort retention and reactivation success

  • Growth Opportunities: Identifies underperforming tiers and provides tier-specific incentive strategies to drive activation and retention

Strategic Insights
This analysis reveals that top-tier users (Whales/Sharks), while fewer, drive the majority of volume and exhibit strong retention. In contrast, low-tier users (Crab/Shrimp) comprise the bulk of the user base but contribute minimally to volume and show higher churn—indicating areas ripe for reactivation and engagement strategies.

Analyzing user and LP behavior side-by-side further exposes overlapping trends and divergence in contribution, enabling Avalanche ecosystem teams to tailor interventions with precision.

This structured approach provides the Avalanche ecosystem with a solid foundation for:

  • Designing better-targeted liquidity mining programs

  • Deploying data-driven, tier-specific marketing campaigns

  • Informing strategic platform feature enhancements

  • Enabling long-term, sustainable growth across its DEX ecosystem.
